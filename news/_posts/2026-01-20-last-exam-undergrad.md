Taking the last exam of my undergraduate journey feels both surreal and deeply meaningful.
{:.lead}

After years of classes, projects, deadlines, presentations, and countless late nights, I have finally completed the last exam of my B.Sc. in **Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Dhaka**. This marks the end of a very important chapter in my undergraduate life and the beginning of a new one.

<!--more-->

One of the most meaningful parts of this journey has been my undergraduate thesis, titled **“Beyond Surface Metrics: Leveraging LLMs to Assess Semantic Depth of Human Responses,”** completed under the supervision of **Dr. Md. Mosaddek Khan**.

At the heart of this thesis was a simple but important question: **can student answers be evaluated in a way that goes beyond surface-level word matching and better reflects their actual meaning?** Traditional evaluation methods often struggle when answers are paraphrased, partially correct, or expressed in different but valid ways. My work focused on building an approach that captures the **semantic depth** of student responses rather than relying only on lexical similarity.

As part of this work, I built **CognifyQ**, a web-based platform for automated assessment that supports both **Bangla and English**. The system is designed to help make assessment more meaningful, scalable, and aligned with human judgment, with a focus on practical use in real educational settings.

Through this project, I learned a great deal about **large language models, semantic evaluation, educational AI, bilingual assessment, and human-aligned system design**. More importantly, I learned how research becomes most valuable when it is not only technically strong, but also practical, accessible, and grounded in real needs.

Completing my last undergraduate exam feels like more than just the end of coursework. It feels like a milestone that reflects years of learning, growth, and exploration. I am deeply grateful to my supervisor, teachers, friends, and family for their support throughout this journey.

As I look ahead, I am excited to continue working on problems related to **AI, natural language processing, educational technology, multilingual systems, and human-centered evaluation**.
