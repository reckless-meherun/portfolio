The thesis, titled **“Beyond Surface Metrics: Leveraging LLMs to Assess Semantic Depth of Human Responses,”** was completed under the supervision of **Dr. Md. Mosaddek Khan**. It focuses on using large language models to move beyond shallow word-overlap metrics and evaluate the deeper semantic quality of student responses in educational settings.

<!--more-->

This work was carried out as part of my B.Sc. journey and reflects my broader research interest in **AI, natural language processing, educational technology, and human-aligned evaluation systems**. The project explores how automated assessment systems can better align with human judgment while remaining practical for real educational use.

A central outcome of this work is **CognifyQ**, a web-based platform for semantic grading of written responses, designed to support both **Bangla and English**. The system aims to make assessment more consistent, scalable, and useful for both teachers and students.

I am deeply grateful to my supervisor, teachers, collaborators, and everyone who supported me throughout this journey. Defending this thesis marks an important milestone in my undergraduate life and a meaningful step toward my future research goals.

## Thesis Details

- **Thesis Title:** Beyond Surface Metrics: Leveraging LLMs to Assess Semantic Depth of Human Responses  
- **Institution:** University of Dhaka  
- **Department:** Department of Computer Science and Engineering  
- **Supervisor:** Dr. Md. Mosaddek Khan  
- **Project Focus:** LLM-based semantic grading, educational AI, bilingual assessment, feedback quality

## Looking Ahead

This thesis has strongly shaped my current research direction. Going forward, I hope to continue working on problems related to **LLM evaluation, educational AI, multilingual NLP, and practical human-centered AI systems**.
